Types of Social Engineering

As long as there is human interaction involved, it can be considered a social engineering attack. A few examples of social engineering are:

  • Phishing - A malicious link or website disguised as a trusted source in an attempt to obtain personal information.
  • Baiting - An enticing prize or device that is a cover for malware.
  • Scareware - Fake alarms and threats sent to a victim to scare them into thinking they have malware on their device. The second part to this attack is asking the victim to download software to remove the malware, the software downloaded is malware itself.
  • Dumpster Diving - When a person searches a company's trash in search for private information that has not been correctly destroyed.

Preventing Social Engineering

Social engineering can be prevented if the following strategies are kept in mind.

  • Stay suspicious about any unknown caller, message, or email.
  • Check a website's security before sending any private information.
  • Use Multi-Factor Authentication.
  • Do not plug in any unknown USB into your device.
  • Keep passwords updated regularly.
  • Destroy any sensitive documents before throwing them away.
